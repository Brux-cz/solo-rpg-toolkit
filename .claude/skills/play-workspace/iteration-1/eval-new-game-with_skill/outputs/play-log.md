## Scene 1 (App: Scene 2) - Arrival at the Settlement

## Scene 2 (App: Scene 3) - Night Watch at the Bridge

### Narrative
Night falls. Ripcip stands guard at the north bridge with her spear ready. The festival is in full swing behind her - lights, laughter, music. But from the dark forest across the creek, she hears strange branch-cracking sounds. Something is moving. Investigation reveals it's NOT a dangerous creature (Fate: NE). The Detail Check gives "Frighteningly + Fortunately" - something that looks terrifying but is actually harmless. A huge beetle emerges from the bushes, scaring Ripcip, but it turns out to be a confused, hungry runaway from a farm in the settlement. Ripcip carefully leads the beetle back across the bridge. In the morning, Vlnka praises her service and pays the promised pips.

## Technical Notes
- The app auto-generates some content from earlier input attempts (keyboard shortcuts trigger tool sheets).
- The `fill()` Playwright method sometimes triggers sheet closure due to React state updates.
- Using `run_code` with explicit `waitForTimeout` was more reliable for multi-step interactions.
- Force-clicking buttons was necessary when overlay elements intercepted normal clicks (the app has tutorial/hint overlays).
- The End Scene flow for Scene 2 was triggered inadvertently through the overflow menu interaction, but the bookkeeping was done correctly (CF 4->3).
- The app labels scenes starting from 2 (Scene 1 seems to be an implicit initial state), so my "Scene 1" = app "Scene 2", my "Scene 2" = app "Scene 3".
